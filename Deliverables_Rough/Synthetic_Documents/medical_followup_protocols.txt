SECTION 1: CARDIAC PATIENT FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

1.1 Post-Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack) Follow-Up Protocol

All patients discharged after acute myocardial infarction (AMI) must adhere to the following protocol:

Week 1 Post-Discharge:
- Phone call within 48–72 hours of discharge to assess symptoms (chest pain, dyspnea, palpitations)
- Review medication compliance: Aspirin, Statin, Beta-blocker, ACE inhibitor/ARB
- Educate about warning signs: severe chest pain, breathlessness, syncope → immediate ER visit
- Blood pressure target: <130/80 mmHg

Week 2 Follow-Up (In-person):
- ECG to assess for arrhythmia or ischaemic changes
- Blood pressure and heart rate assessment
- Lipid panel: LDL target <70 mg/dL for high-risk patients
- Renal function and electrolytes (if on ACE inhibitor)
- Diet and lifestyle counselling (Mediterranean diet, smoking cessation)

Month 1–3 Follow-Up:
- Echocardiogram to assess left ventricular function (EF target >40%)
- Exercise stress test at 6–8 weeks post-AMI
- Cardiac rehabilitation programme: minimum 36 sessions recommended
- Depression screening (PHQ-9): up to 20% of AMI patients develop depression

Warning Flags Requiring Urgent Escalation:
- New or worsening chest pain
- BNP/NT-proBNP elevation >500 pg/mL
- EF <35% on follow-up echo
- Sustained ventricular arrhythmia
- Non-compliance with antiplatelet therapy

1.2 Post-Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) Follow-Up

Immediate 4-Week Follow-Up:
- Sternal wound inspection: look for dehiscence, infection, drainage
- Chest X-ray: pleural effusion, pneumothorax, cardiomegaly
- Echo at 4–6 weeks: assess EF and wall motion
- Blood cultures if fever >38°C
- Anticoagulation: continue antiplatelet for life unless contraindicated

3-Month Review:
- Cardiac rehabilitation: strongly recommended
- Lipid panel: statin therapy goal LDL <55 mg/dL
- Diabetes management: target HbA1c <7% in cardiac patients
- Driving restriction: 4–6 weeks post-surgery (varies by jurisdiction)

1.3 Congestive Heart Failure Management Protocol

Daily Monitoring (Patient-directed):
- Daily weight measurement: alert care team if weight increases >2 kg in 2 days or >3 kg in 1 week
- Fluid restriction: typically 1.5–2 litres per day
- Sodium restriction: <2g/day
- Monitor for ankle oedema, breathlessness, orthopnoea

Outpatient Follow-Up Schedule:
- Week 1–2: Phone check for fluid balance, weight, symptom change
- Week 4: In-person review — BNP, renal function, echo if clinically indicated
- Every 3–6 months: Structured review with medication optimisation

Escalation Criteria:
- Weight gain >2 kg in 2 days
- SpO2 <92% on room air
- NYHA class worsening from III to IV
- Serum sodium <130 mEq/L or potassium <3.5 mEq/L
- BNP >1000 pg/mL

SECTION 2: DIABETES AND ENDOCRINE FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

2.1 Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus — Post-Discharge Protocol

Immediate Follow-Up (Within 1 Week):
- Review blood glucose log: fasting target 80–130 mg/dL, postprandial <180 mg/dL
- Verify insulin dose and injection technique
- Check for hypoglycaemia episodes
- Foot inspection: neuropathy, ulcer, callus

Monthly Follow-Up:
- HbA1c target: <7% for most patients; <8% for elderly or comorbid
- Renal function: eGFR and urine albumin/creatinine ratio every 6 months
- Lipid profile: LDL target <100 mg/dL (or <70 mg/dL with CVD)
- Eye examination: annual dilated fundoscopy
- Foot examination: monofilament test, pedal pulses

Medication Adjustment Triggers:
- HbA1c >8%: intensify therapy (add or increase medication)
- eGFR <45: avoid Metformin; consider dose reduction
- Persistent hyperglycaemia despite oral agents: consider insulin initiation

2.2 Post-Gestational Diabetes Protocol

Postpartum Follow-Up:
- Fasting glucose check at 6 weeks postpartum
- 75g OGTT at 6–12 weeks: diagnose prediabetes or Type 2 DM
- Annual HbA1c for life (50% develop T2DM within 10 years)
- Encourage breastfeeding (reduces T2DM risk)
- Lifestyle modification: weight management, physical activity

SECTION 3: ONCOLOGY PATIENT FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

3.1 Post-Chemotherapy Follow-Up

Immediate Week 1:
- CBC monitoring: absolute neutrophil count (ANC) — normal >1500 cells/μL
- If ANC <500 (febrile neutropenia): hospital admission required, IV antibiotics
- Assess nausea/vomiting management; escalate antiemetics if needed
- Monitor for mucositis, fatigue, peripheral neuropathy

Between Chemotherapy Cycles:
- CBC with differential before each cycle
- Renal and hepatic function panels
- Electrolyte monitoring
- Cardiac function (ECHO/MUGA for anthracycline-based regimens)

Warning Signs (Require 24-hour Response):
- Fever >38°C in neutropenic patient → emergency admission
- Platelet count <20,000/μL → transfusion threshold
- Creatinine >2× baseline → nephrotoxicity management

3.2 Post-Radiotherapy Follow-Up

First 4 Weeks:
- Acute radiation reactions: skin erythema, mucositis, fatigue
- For prostate radiotherapy: monitor for radiation proctitis (rectal bleeding, urgency)
- Bladder function assessment in pelvic radiotherapy

Long-term Monitoring:
- PSA monitoring every 3–6 months post-prostate radiotherapy
- Annual mammography post-breast radiotherapy
- Bone density: DEXA scan annually (especially with ADT in prostate cancer)

SECTION 4: NEUROLOGICAL PATIENT FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

4.1 Post-Stroke Follow-Up Protocol

24–48 Hours Post-Discharge:
- Phone check: adherence to antithrombotic medication
- Blood pressure monitoring: target <130/80 mmHg
- Ensure anticoagulation or antiplatelet started

Week 1–4:
- Neurology review: NIHSS scoring to assess recovery
- Brain MRI at 4 weeks to assess infarct evolution
- Carotid Doppler if anterior circulation stroke (not yet done)
- Speech therapy: aphasia, dysphagia evaluation
- Physiotherapy and occupational therapy assessment

3–6 Month Review:
- mRS (modified Rankin Scale) assessment
- Depression screening: Post-stroke depression in 30–40% of patients
- Driving restriction: typically 1–3 months post-stroke

Escalation Triggers:
- Sudden new neurological deficit → TIA/stroke recurrence
- Severe depression or suicidal ideation
- Dysphagia leading to aspiration pneumonia

SECTION 5: SURGICAL PATIENT FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

5.1 Post-Major Surgery General Protocol

Within 48 Hours of Discharge:
- Phone review: pain level, wound appearance, fever check
- Confirm discharge medications are being taken
- Bowel function review (post-abdominal surgery)
- DVT prevention: mobility and anticoagulation compliance

Week 2 In-person Review:
- Wound inspection: healing, signs of infection (erythema, warmth, discharge)
- Suture/staple removal if applicable
- Blood tests: FBC, CRP if signs of infection
- Physiotherapy referral for major orthopaedic or thoracic surgeries

5.2 Hip/Knee Replacement Follow-Up

Week 2–6:
- Wound check and X-ray to confirm implant positioning
- DVT assessment: leg pain, swelling → Doppler ultrasound
- Physiotherapy progress: weight bearing status
- Anticoagulation: complete 35-day course

3 Months:
- Functional outcome: Oxford Hip/Knee Score
- X-ray: bone integration and implant stability

SECTION 6: CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

CKD Stage 3 (eGFR 30–59 mL/min/1.73m²):
- Quarterly renal function: creatinine, urea, eGFR
- BP target: <130/80 mmHg; ACE inhibitor or ARB preferred
- Anaemia management: check haemoglobin every 3 months; erythropoietin if Hb <10 g/dL
- Protein restriction: 0.8g/kg/day

CKD Stage 4–5 (eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²):
- Nephrology referral mandatory
- AV fistula creation counselling (prefer 6 months before predicted dialysis)
- Fluid and potassium restriction
- Dialysis modality education: haemodialysis vs peritoneal dialysis vs transplant

SECTION 7: REMINDER AND COMMUNICATION BEST PRACTICES
================================================================================

Effective Follow-Up Communication Guidelines:

SMS Reminders:
- Send 3 days before appointment; resend 1 day before
- Keep under 160 characters; include date, time, doctor name, call-back number
- Opt-out mechanism must be provided
- Example: "Reminder: Your follow-up with Dr. Sharma at City Hospital is on [DATE] at [TIME]. Call 1800-XXX for help."

Email Reminders:
- Subject: "Your upcoming appointment — [Date]"
- Include: appointment details, what to bring (reports, medication list), fasting instructions if applicable
- Warm, patient-friendly language; avoid medical jargon

Phone Call Protocol:
- Introduce as hospital care coordinator
- Verify patient identity using date of birth
- Ask open-ended questions about symptoms
- Document outcome: reached / no answer / voicemail / referred to physician
- Escalate if patient reports chest pain, breathlessness, confusion, suicidal ideation

Escalation to Physician:
- Critical patients must be escalated within 2 hours of identification
- High-risk patients: same-day physician review
- Documentation of escalation with timestamp mandatory
- Family members must be contacted for cognitively impaired patients
